Eric Fay is an accomplished security executive with a track record of building and scaling high performing security organizations for fast moving, product led businesses. He brings deep experience across product security, cloud and infrastructure security, application security, and governance, risk, compliance, partnering closely with engineering and product leaders to embed security into how software is designed, built, and shipped. Eric is known for translating risk into clear business outcomes, establishing pragmatic security programs and operating rhythms, and delivering measurable improvements in security posture without slowing innovation.

He currently serves as Senior Director, Head of Information Security at Flatiron Health, responsible for leading the company's security strategy and execution across Security Engineering, Incident Detection and Response, Governance Risk & Compliance and Identity Access Management. Previously, Eric held senior security leadership roles including Head of Security Engineering, Corporate Services, at Amazon, Director of Information Security at Carta, Head of Information Security, Braintree & Swift, at PayPal, and Head of Application Security at Hulu, where he built teams, implemented scalable controls, and partnered with engineering organizations at significant scale.

Across his career, Eric has consistently modernized security programs by prioritizing high leverage controls, automating where possible, and aligning stakeholders around shared metrics—helping organizations accelerate growth while protecting customers, employees, and critical business systems.

What I focus on

  • Security strategy & operating model aligned to business goals and risk appetite.
  • Secure SDLC / shift left tooling and workflows engineers actually use.
  • Cloud & platform security guardrails, identity, scalable governance.
  • Detection & response with clear ownership, playbooks, feedback flywheels.
  • GRC & regulatory execution for data sensitive environments (PHI/PII, PCI, privacy).
  • Metrics that drive action (coverage, time to fix, reduction in blast radius).

How I work

I translate technical risk into business impact, then build durable systems around it: clear standards, automation first controls, and accountability that supports shipping.

  • Simple frameworks for prioritization and decision making.
  • Automation where it matters to reduce toil and improve consistency.
  • Partnership with engineering so security becomes part of how work gets done.
